MoneyMan Privacy Policy

Effective date: 23 July 2026

MoneyMan is a personal finance tracker built by a single independent developer. This policy describes, in plain language, exactly what data the app touches and why.

1. Your financial data stays on your device

Every account, transaction, category, debt, savings goal, recurring rule, and budget you enter is stored only in a local database on your phone. MoneyMan has no server, no user accounts, and no login. The developer cannot see, access, or receive your financial data at any time, because there is no channel for it to travel through.

2. Optional: encrypted backup to your own Google Drive

If you choose to turn on "Back up to Google Drive" in Settings, MoneyMan will:

  • Ask Google to authorize access to a single, narrow permission called drive.appdata — a hidden storage folder that exists inside your own Google Drive account. This folder is not visible in your normal Drive file list and no other app (including ones made by the developer) can read it.
  • Encrypt a copy of your local database with a password you choose, using AES-256-GCM. The password is never transmitted anywhere and is not stored in the backup file itself.
  • Upload that encrypted file to your own Drive's hidden app folder, keeping the 8 most recent copies and deleting older ones automatically.

The developer's app never receives, stores, or has any access to your Drive contents beyond writing to that one hidden folder — it is legally and technically your data, on your account, from end to end. You can turn this off at any time in Settings, which also revokes MoneyMan's Google access and deletes the locally stored backup password.

3. One small, anonymous network request: exchange rates

If you use accounts in more than one currency, MoneyMan fetches current exchange rates once per day from a public, free exchange-rate API (open.er-api.com). This request does not include your name, your financial data, or any other information about you or your device — it is the same request anyone could make from a browser.

4. What MoneyMan never does

  • No advertising, no ad networks, no ad identifiers.
  • No analytics or usage tracking of any kind.
  • No third-party data sharing or selling — there is no third party in the loop.
  • No account creation, no email collection, no login of any kind for using the app itself.

5. Other permissions

  • Notifications — used only for optional, on-device reminders (weekly digest, budget alerts, debt due dates) that you turn on yourself in Settings. No notification content ever leaves your device.
  • Storage access (for manual export/import) — used only when you explicitly choose to export a backup or CSV file, or import an old backup, via the system file picker. MoneyMan never browses your files outside of what you pick.

6. Deleting your data

Uninstalling MoneyMan permanently deletes all local data on that device. If you ever turned on Google Drive backup, you can delete the uploaded backups at any time by turning the feature off in Settings (which deletes MoneyMan's local record of your Drive password) or by revoking MoneyMan's access from your Google Account's "Third-party apps & services" page, which also removes MoneyMan's ability to read or write that hidden folder.

7. Children

MoneyMan is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age — it does not collect information from you at all in the sense of sending it anywhere.
